UPSC Mains Answer Writing Framework

Importance of Answer Writing

The UPSC Mains exam requires candidates to write answers that are structured, precise, and analytical. A well-written answer enhances your score significantly. The key to a good answer is clarity, logical flow, and balanced arguments.

Before writing, analyze the **directive words** in the question:

  • Discuss:   Provide a detailed explanation with different perspectives.
  • Analyze:   Break the topic into parts and examine them critically.
  • Critically Evaluate:   Give both positive and negative aspects and conclude with a balanced opinion.
  • Explain:   Give a clear and straightforward answer with reasons.

Example Question & Model Answer

Question: "What are the causes of urban flooding in India? Suggest measures to mitigate it."



Model Answer

Introduction:

Urban flooding refers to excessive water accumulation in cities due to poor drainage systems and heavy rainfall. The recent floods in Mumbai, Chennai, and Bengaluru highlight the growing concern.

Body:

Causes of Urban Flooding:

  • Unplanned Urbanization:   Encroachments on drainage systems.
  • Poor Drainage Infrastructure:   Clogged stormwater drains.
  • Climate Change:  Increased frequency of heavy rainfall.
  • Loss of Natural Water Bodies:  Reduction of lakes & wetlands.

Mitigation Measures:

  • Better urban planning with climate resilience.
  • Restoring natural drainage systems.
  • Strict implementation of municipal laws.
  • Use of smart technology for flood prediction.

Conclusion:

A holistic approach involving infrastructure development, environmental conservation, and governance is required to prevent urban flooding in India.
